HR must adopt a multi-route talent strategy to build the modern IT skill portfolio…

Four complementary routes to closing the capability gap across AI & Agentic, Data, Platform, Product, Integration and Cyber skill domains

BUILD
Train & Develop Internally

Priority skill domains

AI & Agentic Data Governance Cyber & AI Ethics

Key HR actions

Establish a technology skills academy with structured learning pathways mapped to each skill domain
Partner with platforms (Coursera, AWS, Google Cloud) for vendor-certified training and micro-credentials
Run internal hackathons and AI labs to accelerate applied learning and surface hidden talent
Create 70/20/10 development plans — on-the-job stretch, peer coaching, formal learning
Define a skills taxonomy and run regular capability assessments to track progress and identify gaps

Highest long-term ROI but requires sustained investment and 12–24 month lead times for deep technical skills

PARTNER
Strategic & Ecosystem Partners

Priority skill domains

Platform & SRE Integration & API AI Agents

Key HR actions

Define a strategic partner framework that maps partners to specific skill domain gaps and delivery needs
Embed knowledge transfer clauses in all partner contracts to ensure internal capability builds over time
Create co-delivery models pairing internal staff with partner specialists on live programmes
Establish vendor innovation partnerships (e.g. with hyperscalers) for early access to emerging toolsets
Develop partner performance scorecards that include knowledge transfer and capability uplift metrics

Fastest route to specialist capability; risk of dependency if not paired with active knowledge transfer programme

BORROW
Flexible & Contingent Talent

Priority skill domains

Product & Partner Data Engineering SRE

Key HR actions

Build a pre-qualified contingent talent pool of AI, data and platform specialists for rapid deployment
Establish relationships with specialist technology staffing firms and remote talent platforms
Design staff augmentation playbooks so teams can onboard contingent workers at pace without quality risk
Use interim leaders (e.g. fractional CTOs, AI Directors) to bridge capability gaps during transformation
Actively manage IR35 / contractor compliance and total workforce cost as the liquid workforce grows

Optimises speed and cost flexibility; works best as a bridge while BUILD routes mature over 12–24 months

EMBED
Hire, Rotate & Redesign Roles

Priority skill domains

AI & Agentic Cyber Ethics Product

Key HR actions

Refresh job architectures and role profiles to reflect agentic AI, SRE and product ownership capabilities
Launch targeted external hiring campaigns for scarce skills — AI architects, data engineers, SREs
Introduce internal rotation programmes to move talent across product, data and platform teams
Partner with universities and bootcamps for graduate pipelines in AI, data science and cybersecurity
Redesign reward and career frameworks to retain scarce tech talent in a highly competitive market

Critical for embedding capability long-term; competitive hiring market requires differentiated EVP and speed to offer

↻

HR as a Continuous Capability Engine

As technology evolves from GenAI to Agentic AI (2025+), HR must treat talent strategy as a living system — continuously sensing skill gaps, rebalancing across BUILD / PARTNER / BORROW / EMBED routes, and refreshing role architectures to match the pace of change. A static workforce plan is a capability risk.
