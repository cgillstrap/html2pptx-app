02  AGILE WAYS OF WORKING

Agile ways of working: key enablers

Five capabilities that make agile repeatable across Business and IT

A. Agile methods playbook definition

  • Common language for roles, ceremonies and artefacts
  • Pragmatic guidance for hybrid delivery and governance

B. Agile PPM tooling approach

  • Single source of truth for intake, priorities and delivery
  • Portfolio views of value, capacity, dependencies and risk

C. Agile coaching for Business and IT teams

  • Hands-on support to build habits in the first sprints
  • Coaching for product ownership, teams and leaders

D. Agile training design and delivery

  • Role-based learning paths (PO, SM, team, leadership)
  • Learning-by-doing with scenarios from real work

E. Modern engineering support

  • Quality, automation and CI/CD to increase confidence
  • Reusable patterns, guardrails and communities of practice

Key point

Agile is not a process change alone. These enablers turn intent into a repeatable delivery system.

02  AGILE WAYS OF WORKING

The right PPM toolset enables flow, transparency and governance

Without consistent tooling, agile delivery becomes hard to prioritise, measure and scale

What good looks like

  • A single “front door” for demand and intake
  • One backlog per product, connected to portfolio priorities
  • Capacity, dependencies and risks visible end-to-end
  • Lightweight governance with real-time reporting (not decks)
  • Common metrics for flow, value and predictability

Recommended toolchain (Atlassian)

Jira Software

Team execution, sprint / kanban boards, work tracking

Jira Align

Portfolio planning, value streams, OKRs, funding-to-delivery traceability

Confluence

Playbooks, decision records, product documentation and knowledge base

Jira Service Management

Demand intake, triage and service workflows (front door)

Recommendation: standardise on Atlassian (Jira + Jira Align + Confluence) as the PPM backbone.

Journey to activate the enablers

A phased approach that builds foundations first, then scales with confidence

01

Align & design

Weeks 0–4
  • Confirm operating model and success measures
  • Draft playbook v1 (A) and training/coaching plan (C, D)
  • Select tooling pattern and data model (B)
→
02

Stand up foundations

Weeks 4–10
  • Configure Atlassian stack + reporting (B)
  • Train pilot teams and start coaching (C, D)
  • Establish engineering standards & automation (E)
→
03

Scale & embed

Quarter 2+
  • Expand to additional products and portfolios
  • Evolve playbook and communities of practice (A, C)
  • Embed governance cadence in the tools (B)
→
04

Optimise continuously

Ongoing
  • Measure flow + outcomes; remove blockers
  • Refresh training, coaching and playbook (A, C, D)
  • Invest in engineering excellence and reuse (E)
