Activating agile ways of working
Five enablers that make agile delivery repeatable, transparent and scalable.
A
Agile methods playbook definition
  • Define lifecycle, ceremonies and artefacts.
  • Agree roles, decision rights and governance.
B
Agile PPM tooling approach
  • Create a single ‘front door’ for demand intake.
  • Provide portfolio visibility of priorities and dependencies.
C
Agile coaching for business and IT teams
  • Embed coaching to shape behaviours and habits.
  • Support Product Owners, Scrum Masters and leaders.
D
Agile training design and delivery
  • Role-based training and onboarding pathways.
  • Practical simulations plus tool enablement.
E
Modern engineering support
  • CI/CD, automated testing and DevSecOps guardrails.
  • Engineering standards and communities of practice.

Why the right PPM toolset matters
Tooling is the backbone for shared prioritisation, visibility and predictable delivery at scale.
What good looks like
  • Aligns business and IT on one prioritised backlog.
  • Connects strategy → epics → stories → outcomes.
  • Improves dependency, capacity and risk management.
  • Creates consistent reporting for governance and decisions.
  • Enables teams to stay agile without losing control.
Recommendation: Atlassian toolchain
  • Jira Software — team backlogs and sprint execution.
  • Jira Align — portfolio & program planning and forecasting.
  • Confluence — playbook, decisions and knowledge base.
  • Jira Service Management — intake, triage and service workflows.
  • Bitbucket — code, reviews and CI/CD integration.
Recommendation
Standardise on Atlassian as the agile PPM toolset to enable end-to-end visibility and scalable governance.

Journey to activate the enablers
A practical path from design to scale — activating A–E through phased delivery.
1
Align & design (Weeks 0–2)
  • Confirm ambition, scope and value streams. [A]
  • Outline the ways of working playbook. [A]
  • Agree tooling strategy and initial configuration plan. [B]
→
2
Build foundations (Weeks 3–6)
  • Draft playbook, ceremonies and governance. [A]
  • Configure Jira/Jira Align and demand ‘front door’. [B]
  • Design coaching + training approach and materials. [C][D]
→
3
Pilot & iterate (Weeks 7–12)
  • Run pilots; establish cadence, metrics and feedback loops. [A][C]
  • Refine workflows, dashboards and reporting. [B]
  • Introduce modern engineering practices and standards. [E]
→
4
Scale & sustain (Ongoing)
  • Roll out to additional portfolios and value streams. [A][B]
  • Communities of practice and ongoing coaching. [C]
  • Continuous training and engineering maturity roadmap. [D][E]
